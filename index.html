Don't get caught

Shared traps + the 2026 HGV-ban calendar

Summer Saturdays — especially 18 & 25 Jul — are car-changeover hell on French/German arteries. Sundays are lorry-free = best for big motorway miles. Buy e-vignettes before each border.

⚫

Black Saturday — what it is

France · samedi noir

Bison Futé's worst congestion tier (black) — the peak-summer holiday-changeover Saturdays when all of Europe swaps over at once (the chassé-croisé). The main Med arteries (A7 / A9) gridlock for hours.

Same thing, other countries

Germany — Reisewelle / Stau: autobahns A8 Munich–Salzburg, A7, A93 + the Brenner approach. Italy — esodo estivo: A22 Brennero, A4, the coast. Austria / Switzerland: the Alpine passes — Fernpass, Brenner, Tauern, Gotthard.

2026 dates

Sat 18 & Sat 25 Jul — heavy → black, building toward August.

Why it barely touches Plan D. D's French leg is a NE transit (A26/A4 up to Germany) — not the southbound Med jam. D's only real chokepoints are the Fernpass + Brenner passes. Rule: cross weekday-morning, never on a peak Saturday; be stationary at Predazzo on Sat 18. It's also the exact stop-and-go congestion type behind the family's past rear-end — worth dodging.

🚛

2026 HGV bans (verify hours near the date)

France: Sat 07:00–19:00 (Sat 11, 18, 25 Jul) — Bison Futé black Sat 25 Jul, heavy Sat 18. Italy: Sat 08:00–16:00, Sun 07:00–22:00; Fri 24 & 31 Jul 16:00–22:00. Croatia: coastal Sat 04:00–14:00, Sun 12:00–23:00 (summer 15 Jun–15 Sep). Hungary: Sat 15:00 → Sun 22:00.

🅿️

ZTL — auto-fines

Never drive into Verona or Genova old-town ZTLs (cameras fine you by post). Park peripheral / take the bypass. Venice = zero cars — park Mestre (~€5–15/day), train/bus + vaporetto (~€9.50). Monaco: park Parking des Pêcheurs, never drive the circuit.

🎟️

Vignettes & tolls

Slovenia coastal clip is now FREE (no vignette from 1 Jan 2026 — take the Koper coast road). B: Swiss CHF40 (covers Gotthard) + Slovenian ~€16 + Croatian. C: Austria 10-day €12.80 + Hungary 10-day D1 ~€14 — buy e-vignettes before each border.

The call

D leads · C if cash or energy wins

Plan D (Dolomites) is the lead: it's the adventure that actually fits the kids — Erik's confirmed-favourite Alpine Coaster at Predazzo and Ella's turquoise Lago di Carezza — on the calmest arc of the four, with the most settle days (5) before Csopak, no leg over ~6h, and at roughly the same cost as B (~£2.4–2.9k). One hero day, then drop to the base.

The honest fallback: Plan C (Lean & fast) is ~£500–650 all-in — banking ~£2–3k and the energy for the 7-week stay, calmest and quickest to the base. Plan B (Venice / Lakes) is now only worth it if the Venice nostalgia outweighs Erik's coaster — it never gives him his confirmed hit. Plan A (Monaco Run) is the weakest: most km and cost, and still the Monaco-meltdown structure. Remember: the car's driving either way, so this is purely the outbound-route choice.
