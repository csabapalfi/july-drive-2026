Don't get caught

Black Saturday + the border kit

Summer Saturdays — especially 18 & 25 Jul — are changeover hell on the arteries; Sundays are lorry-free. Neither plan is badly exposed, but here's how each sits.

⚫

Black Saturday — and how each plan dodges it

FR samedi noir / DE Reisewelle / IT esodo: the peak-summer changeover when Europe swaps over at once. The main risk points are the southbound French Med arteries and the Alpine passes.

Plan A on Sat 18

Drives Calais → Stuttgart — German-autobahn NE transit, not the southbound Med jam. Its Alpine crossing falls on the lorry-free Sunday.

Plan B on Sat 18

Home. Crosses the Channel Sunday, then the Alps Monday (weekday) — the whole black-Saturday window is skipped entirely.

The rule either way: cross the Fernpass/Brenner off-Saturday. It's also the exact stop-and-go congestion type behind the family's past rear-end — worth dodging.

🎟️

Vignettes — buy before each border

Online the night before: Austria (10-day), Slovenia, Hungary (10-day D1). Germany is toll-free for cars.

🅿️

ZTL — auto-fines

Don't drive into Bolzano or Trento old-town ZTLs (cameras fine you by post). Stay on the bypass; Predazzo/Val di Fiemme is fine.

🚛

2026 HGV bans (verify near the date)

Italy: Sat 08:00–16:00, Sun 07:00–22:00. Austria: Alpine-pass weekend limits. Hungary: Sat 15:00 → Sun 22:00. Sundays are best for big motorway miles.

The call

A is booked & relaxed · B trades a night for a weekend at home

Plan A (cross Fri 17 eve, as booked) is the unhurried version: four nights, a gentle final run split at Ljubljana, the Alpine crossing on the calm lorry-free Sunday. Nothing to change — it's the default.

Plan B (change the Le Shuttle to Sunday morning) keeps a whole extra weekend at home and saves two hotel nights — but it compresses the drive and ends on a long ~7h Tuesday (Predazzo → base, no overnight). Pick B if the extra home time and lower cost beat the relaxed pace; otherwise A. Either way: base Tue 21, Mon + Tue leave, Csopak Fri 24.
