The call

Both verified. One question left: how much is "no Germany" worth?

Option 1 (German S7) is the low-stress default: fewest hours (~17h), a real buffer day, no forced early starts, two minor chokepoints, meeting handled comfortably from Passau. Its Germany is 700 km of the calmest autobahn available — no Cologne, no Frankfurt, no Munich, no stop-and-go corridors.

Option 2 (Zero-Germany Alpine) delivers 0 km of Germany plus an Innsbruck night and a Lake Garda swim — verified viable end-to-end — at the price of ~5h more driving, three timed chokepoints (Walensee / Lueg / Pack, each dissolved by an early start), a tight Wednesday meeting, and zero slack on check-in day. Fork point: Tuesday night (Metz vs Nancy) — the routes are identical to eastern France, so the family can decide on Tuesday afternoon. If in doubt on the day: Option 1.
